Research & Development

Model Selection & Methodology

Understanding the deep learning pipeline, comparative evaluations, and deployment decisions behind OralVision AI.

Project Overview & Motivation

Oral diseases remain a significant public health issue globally, causing pain, discomfort, and even systemic infections when left untreated. Early identification of conditions like dental caries, calculus build-up, and oral ulcers plays a crucial role in preventing long-term periodontal deterioration.

OralVision AI is built as an educational clinical screening tool to demonstrate how Computer Vision and Transfer Learning can support dental professionals and patients by providing rapid, secondary screenings of visual symptoms.

The Training & Inference Pipeline

The model was trained on a curated dataset containing clinical visual representations of 6 distinct oral conditions. The pipeline progresses through four structured stages:

01

Data Prep

All input clinical images are cleaned, normalized, and resized to 224 × 224 pixels.

02

Augmentation

Rotations, zoom ranges, shifts, and brightness scaling were applied to build model generalization.

03

Feature Ext.

Pre-trained neural weights extracted deep structural boundaries and color parameters.

04

Classification

Softmax top layers map feature sets into the 6 targeted oral disease probability distributions.

Why EfficientNetB0 Was Selected

During the research phase, four neural network architectures were evaluated on identical splits of the dataset (consisting of 3,894 training, 834 validation, and 835 test images). The results show that transfer learning models drastically outperform networks trained from scratch:

Model Architecture Train Accuracy Val Accuracy Test Accuracy F1-Score (Weighted) Status
Custom CNN 78.99% 76.86% 75.81% 0.80 Baseline
MobileNetV2 86.57% 78.78% 81.20% 0.81 Evaluated
ResNet50 88.21% 80.58% 81.68% 0.82 Evaluated
EfficientNetB0 87.42% 82.01% 81.80% 0.81 Deployed

Key Decision Factors: While ResNet50 demonstrated highly competitive accuracies and a slightly better F1-score structure, EfficientNetB0 achieved the highest overall Test Accuracy (81.80%) and the best generalization profile (Validation Accuracy of 82.01%). Its compound scaling architecture makes it lightweight, highly efficient, and ideal for responsive web application hosting.
